Early Years

Charles was born on September 17, 1955, in San Jose, California. He is of French ancestry and speaks French and Spanish fluently. Charles began his studies at the University of California, Berkeley, where he planned to study international law, but he dropped out when his tutor requested him to read the information from his book chapter by chapter.

He had Glossophobia, or the dread of speaking in public. After that, one of his buddies suggested that he take acting classes so that he could face his fears. He was given his first role in “Spoon River Anthology,” delivering a monologue. Charles received his apprenticeship from Berkeley Repertory Theatre in the end. After years of training at Berkeley Repertory, he moved to London to study at the ‘Drama Studio London.’ He discovered his gift for accents and dialects there, among other things. He later joined Berkeley Repertory Theatre after returning to California. For four years in a row, Charles was a founding member of the San Jose Repertory Theatre.

Career

Martinet was told by a friend one day to go to the audition, which would take place at a trade exhibition and need candidates to “speak to people as a plumber.” He came at the last minute, just as the casting directors were wrapping up their work for the day. Fortunately, the director permitted him to audition and informed him that he is an Italian from Brooklyn. Martinet had intended to speak in a stereotypically Italian deep, raspy voice, but he later decided that it would be too harsh for the kids to hear. Then he softened it and made it more friendlier, culminating in Mario’s voice as we know it today. According to Charles, the character of ‘Petruchio’ from William Shakespeare’s “The Taming of the Shrew” served as inspiration for his depiction of Mario.
