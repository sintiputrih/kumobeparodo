Adele reportedly choosing between Park MGM and Caesar's Palace for her Las Vegas residency in 2022

After weeks of speculation, Adele finally confirmed on Tuesday that new music is coming, which may be paired with a Las Vegas residency in 2022.

The 33-year-old London native (real name Adele Laurie Blue Adkins) is reportedly considering a Las Vegas residency next year, according to Billboard. 

The singer is contemplating between two different Las Vegas venues, the Park MGM and the Colosseum at Caesars Palace, though it’s unclear which is the favorite.

Both venues are run by LiveNation and both have open dates to fill in 2022 for a potential residency.

The deal isn’t finalized but the singer has been negotiating for weeks, with the residency also said to cover/represent all of her North American touring plans.

She has not booked any arenas or stadiums for 2022, when her new album, reportedly entitled 30, is slated for release.

Sources claim the singer, ‘likes the idea of having her fans travel to see her in Vegas, where she can keep an apartment, avoid being away from her 8-year-old son for long stretches of time, and easily shuttle back and forth from her home in Los Angeles.’

Both venues seat under 7,000 people, allowing for a much more intimate experience than a large stadium.

The news comes just hours after Adele confirmed that her new single, Easy On Me, will be released on October 15.

While it’s unclear when 30 will ultimately debut, another new report Tuesday from Vulture that claimed Adele is also planning a TV special tied into the new album.

The singer’s team has reportedly approached multiple networks and streaming services for a special, which would likely air by the end of this year.

As for the residency, if Adele does sign on for a string of Las Vegas shows, she’d be just the latest star to do so.
