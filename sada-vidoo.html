Career

As a form of escape from bullying, she began writing and recording songs. As a result, Video decided to pursue a musical career. In the late 1990s, she signed a record deal with Virgin Records Denmark under her given name Nicole and rose to prominence by singing the hook to DJ Aligator’s 2000 single “Lollipop.” The song reached the top ten in Norway and Sweden, and it was the sixth best-selling single in Denmark in 2000. Her debut single, “Hide Away,” was released in 2001, and she followed it up with a string of singles through Virgin until the label dropped her because she wasn’t “pretty enough for the camera.” Video has since concentrated on songwriting.

“Ten Miles,” her first hit as a songwriter, was released by Infernal in 2006. The song charted at number two in Poland and was certified Platinum in Denmark.  Between 2002 and 2007, the artist toured Europe as the lead singer for Snap!  In 2008, she was a finalist on Kanal 5’s Danish vocal talent show Elsk mig I nat. The singer, known as Nicole Stokholm, was a member of the band The Stokholm Promise, which released the charity single “United” in 2010 with Danish rapper M.I.L.O—a former ISAF 9 soldier—in memory of Danish soldiers who died in Afghanistan.

Video relocated to London in 2012, where she began working with British musicians Russ and Chris Ballard. She later took on the persona of Sada Video, a “living doll,” and released her debut album A Story with No End on August 11, 2014, via Target Records. The album received mixed to positive reviews and features three singles: “The Actress,” “China Doll,” and a cover of Pat Benatar’s “Love Is a Battlefield.”  In August of that year, the singer performed at the 2014 edition of Copenhagen Pride.  Video auditioned for the thirteenth series of the British The X Factor in 2016 with her song “Love Is a Battlefield.”
