Evelin Bennett is an American YouTube star and social media influencer. Evelin Bennett is one of the members of the Bennett family, who runs the popular vlogging channel, ‘The Ohana Adventure’.

Early Life and Childhood

Evelin Bennett entered this world on 20th May 2010, in Hawaii, USA. Currently, she is 13 years old and her birth sign is Taurus. She was born to Rachel (mother) and Jase Bennett (father).

Moreover, she has three sisters named Klailea, Rykel, and Cora, and two brothers named Shae and Wyatt. They are part of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.

There is no information related to her educational background. However, as of her age, she might be attending primary school or might be homeschooled. Evelin’s father, Jase, attended Utah Valley University before moving to Hawaii.

He worked as a senior vice president at AMG Hawaii from 2007 to 2010 and as president of Jaseboards North Shore Hawaii from 2010 to 2016. The family relocated to the Continental USA in 2015 and settled in Heber City, Utah.

Professional Life

More about their YouTube channels

The incredible popularity of ‘The Ohana Adventure’ has prompted Evelin, her parents, and her siblings to set up other YouTube channels. Both Klailea and Rykel have their self-titled channels.

And the boys of the family, Shae, and Wyatt run the channel ‘Ohana Boys’ together. Moreover, in April 2019, Rachel set up a channel for herself under the username ‘The Now Mom.’

Evelin and her youngest sister Cora operate ‘Toy Library,’ a channel where they post videos on toy skits, make-believe plays, challenges, and games.

It was created on November 8, 2018, and the first video, titled ‘Thanksgiving 3 Marker Challenge’, was shared on 24 November. In the ensuing months, the channel has garnered tens of thousands of subscribers and millions of views.

Likewise, some of the most popular videos on this channel include ‘The First Day at the Toy Salon!’, ‘Toy Vault Escape Room Challenge! Stuck inside secret toy Vault!’, and ‘We Woke Up as Leprechauns! I hope no one Catches us!’
