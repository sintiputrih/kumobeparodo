Alaskan Bush People's late Billy Brown 'communicates from the beyond' to his kids on the radio, daug

ALASKAN Bush People's late Billy Brown still communicates with the family, according to his daughter Rain.

The 18-year-old revealed that her father gives her "signs from beyond" one year following his tragic death.

In an interview with Hollywood Life, the reality star opened up about how the family is grieving after the passing of Billy.

“He absolutely gives me signs from beyond. 100 percent. Some of these signs are so blatantly obvious and theses signs happen to all of us almost every day,” Rain told the publication.

“It makes me feel better knowing that he is always with me. It is very comforting," she added.

The youngest Brown sibling explained the "signs" she gets from her dad who died in February after suffering a seizure in the family's Washington home.

“He comes through on the radio," she recalled. "It’s so strange because, even on the stations that don’t usually play this kind of music, I will turn on that station, or be flipping through the channels, you hear Stevie Ray on a station that does not play that kind of music."

She continued: "Or a song that will describe what is happening in my life. It will come on at the exact moment I am thinking about it or thinking about him. It is too strange to be coincidence."

The matriarch's final moments and funeral arrangements appeared on the Discover show in a recent episode.

Billy's son, Bear, 34, explained to HollywoodLife why the family chose to air such intimate moments saying: “Dad loved the show and he loved the people so much!

“We think that the fans and everyone that loves the show deserves to see everything and to see his funeral and all.

“We discussed whether or not to include it and we felt like we should include it for sure because we believe that is what Dad would want.”

Bear also explained how he is doing in the death's aftermath: “I am doing alright. You think that you understand loss.

"I am just trying to keep one foot in front of the other.”

Reflecting on his father’s passing, Bear said: “That was the hardest episode I have ever filmed.

“I broke down several times during the filming of this.

“My dad meant so much to me.

“I should have seen it coming because he had failing health for a while.

“It just seemed really sudden and out of the blue.”

TRAGIC PASSING

On the October 31st episode, Billy's daughter Snowbird, 26, got emotional about the moment she realized she was going to lose her father.

Snowbird recalled being told by emergency services that they couldn't put Billy in the helicopter because his heart was too weak.

She teared up as she remembered the family were told to say their goodbyes when Billy was still alive.

"The helicopter came and they assessed it and he was too bad to take him in the helicopter.

"They couldn’t continue CPR on him or take him anywhere because his heart wouldn’t beat on its own.

"So they told us while he was still alive that we should say our goodbyes."

Later on Snowbird confided: "I know dad would want for us not to be sad, and to go on. But honestly going forward seems impossible because every day seems like a nightmare that I cant wake up from. Or hell."

The latest episode covered the events of that day and the void that Billy left behind as his wife and kids struggled to come to terms with his loss.

Billy also shared kids Matthew, 38, Joshua "Bam Bam" Brown, 37, Gabe, 31, and Noah, 28, with wife Ami, 58.

DEVASTATING LOSS

Footage from the incident features members of the family shouting: "Wake up dad!"

As The Sun previously reported, CPR was performed unsuccessfully.
