About Deion Sanders: A Brief Bio

Deion Sanders better known as Neon Deion, Prime Time, and Coach Prime is a retired American Football and Baseball star and a superstar College Football coach.

Born Deion Luwynn Sanders Sr. on August 9, 1967, the player was inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame and the College Football Hall of Fame in 2011. He is the only athlete to play in both a Super Bowl (for American Football) and a World Series (for Major League Baseball).

Career Highlights

  • Sanders won two Super Bowl titles during his NFL career, with the San Francisco 49ers in Super Bowl XXIX and with the Dallas Cowboys in Super Bowl XXX.
  • Sanders was selected to eight Pro Bowls during his NFL career.
  • He was named First-Team All-Pro six times in his NFL career.
  • Sanders won the Jim Thorpe Award as a senior at Florida State University.
  • He is widely regarded as the greatest cornerback in NFL history.
  • He played for five different NFL teams during his career: Atlanta Falcons, San Francisco 49ers, Dallas Cowboys, Washington Redskins, and Baltimore Ravens.
  • Sanders played Major League Baseball for nine seasons, for the New York Yankees, Atlanta Braves, Cincinnati Reds, and San Francisco Giants.
  • Worked as an NFL analyst for NFL Network, CBS/CBS Sports Network, and ESPN.
  • Coached college football at Jackson State University and the University of Colorado Boulder
