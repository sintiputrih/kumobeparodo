Professional Career of Dearica Hamby:

College Career at Wake Forest

Hamby was a member of the Wake Forest Demon Deacons from 2011 to 2015. In the Atlantic Coast League, she was the top player (ACC). She dominated the nation’s top league in scoring and rebounds as a junior. It was significant to be the first Demon Deacon to lead the ACC in both categories in the same season.

She also became Wake Forest’s all-time leading scorer and rebounder with 1,801 points and 1,021 rebounds. She was included in the first ACC players’ team and considered for the John R. Wooden Award, which is given to the nation’s top female collegiate basketball player.

WNBA Career with San Antonio Stars / Las Vegas Aces

In the 2015 WNBA draft, the San Antonio Stars selected Hamby with the sixth overall choice. She was a member of the Stars until their 2018 relocation to Las Vegas and rebranding as the Aces.

After developing into one of the league’s top bench players, Hamby was twice awarded the WNBA Sixth Woman of the Year title, in 2019 and 2020. She also participated in two WNBA All-Star games in 2021 and 2022.

She is also most remembered for her clutch play in the second round of the 2019 WNBA playoffs against the Chicago Sky, where she made a game-winning basket from almost half-court. The shot, known as “the Hamby Heave,” proved to be one of the most iconic in WNBA history and helped the Aces win.

The pinnacle of Hamby’s career came in 2022 when she assisted the Aces in winning their first WNBA championship. She averaged 11.8 points, 7.8 rebounds, and 2.0 assists per game in the playoffs. She was crucial in the Finals against the Connecticut Sun. Also, she and the Aces won the Commissioner’s Cup that same year.

WNBA Career with Los Angeles Sparks

In February 2023, Hamby was traded to the Los Angeles Sparks. He was a part of an enormous group that also featured Nneka Ogwumike and Liz Cambage. Given that Hamby having recently inked a multi-year contract extension with the Aces in June 2022, many fans and observers were taken aback by the transaction.

She claimed she was traded without her knowledge or consent while expecting her second child when she sued the Aces and the WNBA in September 2023 for discrimination. She claimed that she was mistreated by the Aces administration and that they prevented her from receiving the necessary prenatal treatment.

Hamby returned to the court with the Sparks for the 2023–2024 campaign, forming a tandem with Azura Stevens and the Ogwumike twins. Her 8.9 points, 5.9 rebounds, and 1.8 assists per game were crucial in the Sparks’ fourth-place Western Conference finish and postseason berth.

In her final game of the season, a September 3 victory over the Washington Mystics, the WNBA player recorded a double-double. She pulled down ten rebounds and scored twelve points.
