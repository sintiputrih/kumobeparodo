Charity

Slim and his brother are frequently involved in community service through various causes.

Cash Money Records has donated more than $225,000 to “Forward Together New Orleans” (FTNO), a 501(c)(3) charity organization dedicated to closing critical gaps in community needs and available resources to defend from the COVID-19 pandemic.

In addition to paying June’s rent, Cash Money has teamed up with iHeart Media’s Q93 and WYLD radio stations in New Orleans to give away a hundred $100 gift cards for groceries to radio listeners every weekday in May, totaling $10,000.

Book Publications

In 2010, Cash Money Content was launched as an extension of the Cash Money Records brand.

Williams and his brother founded Cash Money Content, including a book publishing company and a film production company.

The book sector of the company was built in collaboration with Simon & Schuster’s Atria Books subsidiary, which served as the major distributor.

Through Cash Money Content, Williams has been involved in community-focused activities.

Other ventures

Ronald “Slim” Williams and his brother Bryan “Baby” Williams founded the oil exploration company “Bronald Oil” in 2010.

An investigation into the company by Bloomberg in 2010 discovered significant contradictions between the company’s statements and its real operations in the primary locations targeted on their website.

Unfortunately, the publication of this report coincided with the website’s removal from the internet at the time.

Williams’ company was re-registered in 2013, and it began operating without the fanfare and announcements that accompanied the company’s 2010 launch.

Presently, the website is no longer operating, and the company is no longer mentioned by Williams and his associates at Cash Money Records.

Career

In 1991, Ronald and Birdman formed Cash Money Records. They began their business with money borrowed from their father.

Cash Money struck a $30 million pressing and distribution deal with Universal in 1998, entitling the label to 85% of royalties, 50% of publishing earnings, and control of all masters.

Lil Wayne, Drake, Nikki Minaj, Tyga, and Bow Wow are just a few of the rap stars who have signed to Cash Money Records.

Slim owns a clothing line called YMCMB, which stands for Young Money Cash Money Billionaires. He also owns the GT Vodka liquor brand.

Slim and Birdman were featured in CNBC’s documentary “Newbos: The Rise of America’s New Black Overclass,” highlighting a few black multimillionaires in 2009.
