Michael Rapaport | Career

Rapaport traveled to Los Angeles when he was 19 years old in the hopes of becoming a successful stand-up comedian. His stepfather assisted him in landing a job as a comedian.

With the series ‘China Beach,’ he made his television debut in 1990. Rapaport later appeared in the episode ‘One Small Step’ as ‘Kravitz.’

He starred as ‘Gary’ in four episodes of the hit NBC comedy ‘Friends‘ in 1999. A year later, he starred alongside Arnold Schwarzenegger as ‘Adam’ in the film ‘The 6th Day.’

The next year, Rapaport appeared in the comedy ‘Dr. Dolittle 2‘ as ‘Joey,’ a raccoon. Michael joined the cast of the American sitcom ‘The War at Home,’ where he portrayed the lead role of ‘David Gold,’ in 2005.

The series followed a dysfunctional family as they struggled to overcome their difficulties.

Rapaport did a fantastic job in the role and stayed with the show till it ended in 2007. Michael Rapaport starred as ‘Jason Mullins’ in the action comedy film ‘The Heat’ in 2013.

Similarly, Rapaport landed the role of ‘Doug Gardner’ in the Netflix series ‘Atypical’ in 2017.
