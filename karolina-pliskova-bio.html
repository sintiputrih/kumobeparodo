Karolina Pliskova: Career

Before even starting her professional career, Pliskova was already a junior Grand Slam champion.

To explain, the 31-year-old won the 2010 Australian Open junior title by defeating Laura Robson in the final.

As a result, Karolina developed a taste for winning very early on in her career.

After that, the Czech tennis star’s first Grand Slam appearance came during the 2012 French Open, where she reached the first round. Likewise, Pliskova won her maiden WTA title at the Malaysian Open in 2013.

In the final, the 31-year-old defeated Bethanie Mattek-Sands to start a title-winning spree that has seen Karolina win 16 singles titles as of now.

After that, Pliskova’s next big career highlight happened during the 2016 U.S Open, where she reached her first Grand Slam final.

On her way to the final, the Czech native knocked out extremely tough opposition. None more so than 23-time Grand Slam champion Serena Williams.

However, in the finale, Karolina had a breakdown as she lost the match from a very favorable position.

Nonetheless, Pliskova bounced back from her misery and regained her winning ways. As a matter of fact, the 31-year-old even became world no.1 in WTA rankings on June 17, 2017, a feat that only a handful of ladies have been able to achieve in the past.

Moving onto her most recent Grand Slam appearance, Karolina defeated Kristina Mladenovic and Laura Siefemund in the first and second rounds, respectively, of the 2020 Australian Open.

Consequently, everything seemed pointed toward a deep run in the tournament.

However, the 31-year-old unexpectedly fell to Anastasia Pavlyuchenkova in the third round to everyone’s disappointment.

Nevertheless, we expect Karolina to bounce back and win her maiden Grand Slam title very soon.

Altogether, Pliskova has bagged victories of 16 singles and five doubles titles on the WTA tour, and 10 singles, and six doubles titles on the ITF. Furthermore, Pliskova has had a new coach since 2020. She has hired Naomi Osaka‘s former coach Sascha Bajin.
