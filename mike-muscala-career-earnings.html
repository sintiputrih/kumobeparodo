Discover Mike Muscala Career Earnings: How Rich Is The Wizards Power Forward?

Mike Muscala is one of the leading men of the National Basketball Association‘s Washington Wizards. He plays as a center forward and has been active as an NBA player since 2013.

So, let’s dive into Mike Muscala career earnings and explore this outstanding athlete’s financial status. From an impressive NBA resume to astounding contracts and stats, we will discuss Muscala’s career highlights in detail.

Meet the American Basketball Genius

  • Mike was born on July 1, 1991, in St. Louis Park, Minnesota, United States of America. His full name is Michael Peter Muscala. He is a tall player at 6 feet 10 inches in height and weighs 240 pounds.
  • Muscala’s parents are Bob Muscala and Mary Maida. Sadly, Mary died in 2022 after battling cancer for nearly two years. Mike revealed that his mother was his great supporter and fan.
  • The American player has not mentioned much about his family, but he does have a stepsister. He spent his childhood in Minnesota and graduated from Roseville High School.
  • Mike Muscala played for the Bucknell Bison men’s basketball team from 2009-2013 during college. He graduated from Bucknell University with a bachelor’s degree in business administration and Spanish in 2013.
  • After Bucknell retired Muscala’s number 31 jersey on February 16, 2020, he became the third athlete in school history to receive such honor.

Multiple Awards and Achievements Are Testament To His Success

Over the years, the American national has been the recipient of many awards. He has been a star player in the game, and his performances have granted him huge accolades.

Mike was awarded AP All-American in 2011 and 2013 for his contribution to Bucknell’s basketball team. Furthermore, he became Patriot League Player of the Year two times during his collegiate days.

Muscala scored a career-high 19 points in a 129-122 loss against the Chicago Bulls on March 11, 2018. On March 28, 2018, in a 126-114 loss to the Minnesota Timberwolves, he set a new career best and led all scorers with 24 points.
