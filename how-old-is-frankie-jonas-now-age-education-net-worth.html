How old is Frankie Jonas?

Franklin Nathaniel Jonas was born in Wyckoff, New Jersey USA, on 28 September 2000 – his zodiac sign is Libra and he holds American nationality. He is an actor who is perhaps still best known for his portrayal of Frankie Lucas in the comedy family musical series “Jonas”, which aired in 2009 and 2010, and was created by Michael Curtis and Roger S. H. Schulman. The series starred Kevin Jonas, Chelsea Kane and Nicole Gale Anderson, and follows The Jonas Brothers during their everyday lives – it won five awards and was nominated for seven others including one Primetime Emmy.

Childhood and educationand

Frankie was raised in Wyckoff alongside his three older brothers Kevin, Joe and Nick, by their parents who are of very mixed European, French-Canadian and American Cherokee. Their father Paul Kevin Jonas Sr. was a musician and an ordained minister of the Assemblies of God Church, while their mother Denise Marie was a singer and a sign language teacher. All three of Frankie’s brothers are actors and musicians, who are best known for having founded the band The Jonas Brothers, and because Frankie isn’t a member of the band, he is often referred to as ‘The Bonus Jonas’.

Frankie became interested in acting at a very early age, as he was motivated by his parents and brothers, and so joined his elementary school’s drama club at the age of seven – he actually launched his acting career when he was nine. He was interested in sports while attending high school, as he played both football and soccer, and upon matriculation in 2018, decided not to pursue a college degree but to focus on his career as an actor.

Career as an actor

Frankie aforementioned debut was in 2008, when he was invited to voice Sosuke, one of two lead characters in the English version of the Japanese animated movie “Ponyo”, created by Studio Ghibli – the film was both written and directed by Hayao Miyazaki, and the English version starred Cate Blanchett, Matt Damon and Liam Neeson. Frankie also sang the song “Ponyo on the Cliff by the Sea” in the movie, which follows a five-year old boy who meets Ponyo, a goldfish princess who becomes human after developing feelings for Sosuke – it won 12 awards and was nominated for 20 others.

In 2009, Frankie made an uncredited appearance when he portrayed The Child in Museum in the critically acclaimed adventure comedy film “Night at the Museum: Battle of the Smithsonian”, which starred Ben Stiller and Owen Wilson. In 2010, he played Junior Rocker Trevor in the musical comedy film “Camp Rock 2: The Final Jam”, which starred Demi Lovato, Joe and Nick Jonas. In 2011, Frankie voiced Pip in the family fantasy film “Spooky Buddies”, and three of his most recent roles have been in the 2012 animated family musical movie “The Reef 2: High Tide” in which he voiced Junior, the episodes “Coat Rack Cowboy” and “Pumpkinhead” of the action adventure series “R. L. Stine’s The Haunting Hour” in 2013, and the family film “Gutsy Frog” in which he starred as Frankie in 2013.

Other credits

Frankie has appeared in many documentaries, talk shows and TV specials. In 2008, he was in 10 episodes of the biographical TV series “Jonas Brothers: Living the Dream”, and then in the documentary movie “Jonas Brothers: The 3D Concert Experience.

The year 2009 saw him featured in the TV special “The Teen Choice Awards 2009”, at which he won an award for Choice TV Breakout Star: Male. In 2010 he appeared in the documentary film “Ponyo: Behind the Microphone – The Voices of Ponyo”, the TV special “Nickelodeon Kids’ Choice Awards 2010”, and the talk show “Entertainment Tonight”.

In 2011 he was in the episode “Disney Dream” of the documentary series “Disney 365”, and the same year saw him appear in the short documentary “On the Spot Interviews”.

Three of Frankie’s most recent roles have been in single episodes of the talk show “TinaQ’s Celebrity Interviews” in 2013, the news talk show “Good Morning America” in 2016, and the documentary film “Chasing Happiness” in 2019.

Love life and girlfriend

Frankie has been in several relationships, allegedly from the age of seven. His first girlfriend was Noah Cyrus, an American singer and actress whom he dated from 2007 to mid-2009, and two months after their break up, he started seeing the singer Evie Rupp.

After their split in the summer of 2010, Frankie began dating Maya Kibbel who is a non-celebrity girl.

The last Frankie’s relationship whom the public knows about was with Austin, with whom he went to her prom – they dated for several years before splitting ways. Several of his fans claim to have seen Frankie walking hand in hand with his new girlfriend in the streets of Los Angeles in 2017, but as Frankie hasn’t talked about any other girls he might have been with, he is single as of October 2020, hasn’t married and doesn’t have children.

Hobbies and other interests

Frankie likes to play football, and played center for the school’s team Jr. Hornets  – the New York Yankees baseball team is his favorite.

Frankie likes to sing and is talented just as his brothers, and founded his own band Hollywood Shakeup in 2017.

He is physically very active, as he has several training sessions at the gym each week, while he is also following a somewhat strict diet, but allows himself one cheat meal per week, for which he usually chooses cake and pancakes.

Frankie has his own favorite actors and actresses, some of whom are Al Pacino, Robert De Niro and Meryl Streep, and a few of his favorite movies are “Scarface”, “The Sleepers” and “Kramer vs. Kramer”. He also likes to watch animated TV series, and two of his favorites are “The Simpsons” and “South Park”.

Age and net worth

Frankie is 20 years old. He has short brown hair and brown eyes, his height is 5ft 11ins (1.8m) and he weighs around 165lbs (75kgs).

As of October 2020, his net worth has been estimated at over $500,000.
