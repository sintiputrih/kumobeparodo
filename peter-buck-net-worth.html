Peter Buck’s Entrepreneurial Ventures

Peter Buck’s entrepreneurial spirit goes beyond his successful music career. One of his most remarkable achievements is co-founding the Subway fast-food restaurant chain with Fred DeLuca. Buck’s initial investment of $1,000 assisted DeLuca in opening a sandwich shop to fund his college education. Over time, this small establishment, originally named “Pete’s Super Submarines,” transformed into the globally recognized Subway franchise we know today. In collaboration, Buck and DeLuca established “Doctor’s Associates” to oversee the expanding business. Subway has since become one of the largest fast-food chains worldwide, greatly contributing to Buck’s financial success.
